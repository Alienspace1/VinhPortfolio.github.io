Exploring Optical Microscopy Methods to Measure Size Distribution of Airborne Particle

This project investigates optical microscopy for measuring airborne particle size distribution, focusing on particle size calibration and improving imaging with microscopy, ImageJ, and the SPS30 sensor, aiming to simplify cleanroom monitoring and compliance testing while ensuring accuracy.

Figure 1: Microscopy methods measurement with SPS30 PM sensor and
HC-05 Bluetooth setup inside box.

Figure 2: Project poster.

Abstract: Cleanrooms are specialized environments designed to regulate environmental variables, such as airborne particulate matter (PM), humidity, and temperature. To ensure compliance with international cleanroom standards, accurate measurements of airborne particle counts and sizes are crucial. However, existing measurement instruments are often expensive. Exploring alternative methods for acquiring particle distribution data could significantly reduce the cost of routine cleanroom monitoring. One such method involves using optical lenses and cameras. The objective of our project was to develop and investigate methodologies for measuring particle sizes using optical microscopy. A novel particle sizing system based on optical principles was designed, and such an instrument could substantially lower the costs of cleanroom compliance testing.

Keywords - PM (particulate matter,) cleanrooms, microscopy
